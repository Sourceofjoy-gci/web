MASTER OF THE HIGH COURT

About the Office of the Master

The Office of the Master of the High Court helps administer and protect assets when someone passes away, when companies close down, or when people face financial difficulties. We serve as guardians of the legal process to ensure that everyone's rights are protected during these challenging times.

Our Responsibilities

What the Master's Office handles

Deceased Estates

We administer the estates of people who have passed away, ensuring their property and assets are distributed according to their will or the law.

Protection of Interests

We protect the interests of creditors, debtors, legatees, minors, children, and heirs in the estate administration process.

Wills

We handle the registration and safekeeping of wills to ensure they are properly executed when the time comes.

Insolvent Estates & Liquidations

We oversee the winding up of insolvent estates and the liquidation of companies to ensure fair treatment of all parties.

How to Report an Estate

A simple step-by-step guide

Step 1

Time Frame

Report the estate within 14 days of the person's passing.

Any person who was present at the time of death, is in charge of the house where the death occurred, or is owed by the deceased can report the estate.

Step 2

Where to Report

Visit any of these offices:

  • Master of the High Court Headquarters (Mbabane)
  • Master's Regional Offices
  • Regional Administrator's Office
Step 3

Bring Documents

Bring all required documents (see checklist below)

All copies must be certified

Step 4

Next-of-Kin Meeting

Attend a meeting where an executor/trix will be nominated to wind up the estate

Required Documents

Documents to bring when reporting an estate

Death Certificate
Marriage Certificate (if applicable)
Will (if available)
Decree of Divorce (if applicable)
Birth Certificate
Adoption Certificate (if applicable)
Bank Book/Bank Cards
Share Certificate(s) (if applicable)
Blue Book (vehicle registration)
Title Deed (if applicable)
Revenue Stamps
National Identity Card

All copies of documents must be certified. If you're unsure which documents apply to your situation, please contact the Master's Office for guidance.

Winding Up an Estate

The executor's duties and process

01

Nomination of Executor

An executor/trix is nominated at a next-of-kin meeting to wind up the deceased's property.

02

Asset Assembly

The executor assembles and takes charge of all assets belonging to the deceased.

03

Advertisement

The executor advertises for debtors and creditors of the estate to come forward.

04

Inventory Completion

The executor supplements the inventory with all identified assets.

05

Debt Collection

The executor collects from debtors who owed the deceased.

06

Account Preparation

The executor prepares and files the Liquidation and Distribution Accounts.

07

Payment and Distribution

The executor pays the deceased's debts and distributes the remainder to the heirs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about estates and the Master's Office

What happens if someone dies without a will?

If someone dies without a will (intestate), their assets will be distributed according to the laws of intestate succession in Eswatini. Generally, this means the estate goes to the closest relatives in a specific order, typically starting with the spouse and children. The Master's Office will guide the process to ensure it follows the legal requirements.

How long does it take to wind up an estate?

The time to wind up an estate varies depending on its complexity. Simple estates can be wound up in about 6 months, while more complex estates might take a year or more. Factors that affect the timeline include the size of the estate, whether there's a valid will, any disputes among beneficiaries, and outstanding debts or assets that are difficult to value.

Can I register my will with the Master's Office?

Yes, you can register your will with the Master's Office for safekeeping. This service helps ensure that your will is found and properly executed after your passing. To register your will, bring the original document to any Master's Office location along with your ID. The office will provide you with a receipt confirming the registration.

What are the costs involved in reporting an estate?

There is a nominal fee for reporting an estate, which covers administrative costs. Additionally, you will need to purchase revenue stamps for various documents. The exact costs depend on the size and complexity of the estate. The Master's Office can provide a current fee schedule upon request.

Can the Master's Office help with writing a will?

The Master's Office does not draft wills, but they can provide general guidance on will requirements. For creating a will, it's advisable to consult with a legal practitioner who specializes in estate planning. This ensures that your will meets all legal requirements and clearly expresses your wishes.
