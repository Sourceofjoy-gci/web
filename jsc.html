JUDICIAL SERVICE COMMISSION

Welcome to the Judicial Service Commission of Eswatini

The Judicial Service Commission (JSC) is an independent body established under Section 159 of the Constitution of Eswatini. Think of us as the guardians who help maintain a fair and efficient judicial system in our country.

In simple terms, we make sure the right judges and court officials are appointed, that they follow proper standards, and that the courts run efficiently for all citizens.

What We Do

Our role in simple terms

Select the Right People

We advise the King on who should be appointed as judges and other judicial officers. This helps ensure that only qualified, fair, and independent people serve in our courts.

Maintain Standards

We oversee disciplinary matters for judicial officers. This means if a judge doesn't follow proper conduct, we have a process to address it, helping maintain public trust in the courts.

Review Working Conditions

We look at the terms of service for judges (like salaries and benefits) and make recommendations. This helps attract and retain qualified professionals to serve as judges.

Listen to Feedback

We receive and process recommendations and complaints about the judiciary. This gives citizens a voice and helps us continuously improve the court system.

How We Work

Understanding our process

01

Identify Needs

We identify when new judicial appointments are needed or when issues need to be addressed.

02

Gather Information

We collect all relevant information, whether for appointments, complaints, or improvements.

03

Deliberate

The Commission meets to discuss matters thoroughly and make objective decisions.

04

Recommend

We provide our recommendations to the appropriate authorities (like the King or government).

05

Follow Up

We ensure that recommendations are implemented and continue to monitor effectiveness.

Who We Are

The composition of the Commission

Our Members Include:

  • The Chief Justice (Chairperson)
  • Two legal practitioners of not less than seven years practice
  • The Chairman of the Civil Service Commission
  • Two persons appointed by the King

This diverse composition ensures balanced decision-making that considers different perspectives.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the JSC

What is the main purpose of the Judicial Service Commission?

The main purpose of the JSC is to safeguard the independence of the judiciary by handling appointments, promotions, and disciplinary matters of judicial officers, ensuring that qualified individuals serve in the courts and that proper standards are maintained.

How can I submit a complaint about a judicial officer?

To submit a complaint about a judicial officer, you should write a formal letter addressing it to the Secretary of the Judicial Service Commission. The letter should clearly state the nature of your complaint, providing relevant details such as dates, case numbers, and specific concerns. You can submit this letter at the JSC offices or through the official email address.

Does the JSC appoint all court officials?

No, the JSC does not directly appoint court officials. Rather, it advises the King on appointments of judges and other senior judicial officers. Administrative staff and some lower court officials may be appointed through other government processes.

How often does the JSC meet?

The JSC typically meets quarterly, but can convene additional meetings when necessary to address urgent matters. The schedule of meetings is determined by the Chairperson based on the volume and urgency of issues requiring the Commission's attention.
